# Wiki: Machine Learning

**Machine Learning (ML)** is a specialized subfield of **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** focused on the development of systems capable of learning from data rather than relying solely on explicit programming.

---

## Core Concepts

### 1. Learning Paradigms
Machine learning systems are generally categorized by how they process data to derive insights:

*   **Supervised Learning:** A paradigm that utilizes **labeled data** to train models. The system learns to map inputs to specific, known outputs by being provided with the correct answers during the training process.
*   **Unsupervised Learning:** A paradigm that operates on **unlabeled data**. The system attempts to identify inherent structures, groupings, or patterns within the data without prior guidance or pre-defined labels.

### 2. Neural Networks
**Neural Networks** are a class of computational models inspired by the structure and function of biological neurons. They are fundamental to modern deep learning and operate through a layered architecture:

*   **Structure:** Composed of multiple layers of interconnected nodes (neurons).
*   **Function:** Input data passes through these layers, undergoing a series of transformations that extract increasingly complex features to generate an output.
